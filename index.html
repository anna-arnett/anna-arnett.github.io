Experience

SUPREME Research Scholar - 2024/2025 School Year

After my work concluded this summer, I was selected to receive the Microelectronics Fellowship Award from the SUPREME Research Center. I will continue my work with Dr. Niemier throughout the upcoming school year.

Research Intern at the University of Notre Dame - Summer 2024

This summer, I collaborated with amazing Notre Dame professors and PhD students to study Bayesian neural networks. BNNs provide a measure of uncertainty in their predictions, which lets us know how confident the model is in its output. I helped to develop and train models using PyTorch and TensorFlow, focusing on adding Bayesian layers to Convolutional neural networks.

I also developed a data visualization tool in Python that reads data from an Excel spreadsheet. This contributes to benchmarking efforts of the research group by facilitating easy monitering of key performance indicators. We were able to present this tool to liaisons from Intel and the SUPREME research center, garnering further interest as well as great feedback.

Software Systems Engineering Intern at NASA - Summer 2023

I worked at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center on the GMSEC (Goddard Mission Services Evolution Center) Team. GMSEC is a software framework that facilitates standardized messaging between space mission systems. This was an amazing first foray into the professional software engineering world! I gained experience in deploying software in Agile-based environments as well as reading/writing technical documentation.

Much of my project was focused on creating a deployment strategy using Robot Framework, Docker, and Selenium that could be expanded upon to fit any test scenario. This way, the GMSEC team could have a standardized strategy when it comes to developing test scripts for software suites of containers.

ProtoSat Software Engineer at IrishSat - Year-Round

IrishSat is Notre Dame's space systems design team. We are working towards launching ND's first satellite! I am a member of the Computing Team, and we collaborate with a range of other disciplines (Engineering, Business, Physics, etc.) to fulfill all software needs for the team. This has included network programming, translating code from Python to C, and doing research on the Kalman Filter. We also have a banquet at the end of every year where we present our progress to the Notre Dame community and beyond!

Student Analyst at Lucy Institute of Data Science - Spring 2023
